## From Avionics to Algorithms

After 21 years as a Warrant Officer in the Canadian Armed Forces — specializing in avionics maintenance, quality auditing under AF9000+, and running the instruction cell — I made a deliberate pivot into AI governance and consulting.

It wasn't a random career change. The same skills that kept aircraft systems safe and compliant — systematic auditing, process discipline, risk management, and training others to follow frameworks — are exactly what organizations need as they adopt AI.

## My Approach

> "I don't sell AI hype. I help organizations figure out where they actually stand, what regulations apply to them, and how to build internal processes that make AI work long-term."

Every engagement starts with understanding your context — not pushing a pre-built solution. I believe that good AI governance should be:

**Practical** — frameworks that your team can actually follow, not shelfware.

**Proportionate** — controls matched to your risk level, not one-size-fits-all.

**Progressive** — designed to evolve as your AI maturity and regulations evolve.
