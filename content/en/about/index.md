## What I Do

I help organizations adopt AI responsibly. That means structured maturity assessments, governance frameworks that work in practice, and compliance guidance grounded in how your organization actually operates. Not theory. Not slide decks that sit on a shelf.

My clients are typically Quebec and Canadian organizations (50 to 500 people) trying to figure out where they stand with AI, what regulations apply to them, and how to move forward without creating risk they can't manage.

---

## Where This Comes From

Before AI governance, I spent 21 years in the Canadian Armed Forces. I started as an avionics technician on CF-188 Hornets, moved into electronic warfare, then shifted to quality management as an AF9000+ Lead Auditor. I finished as a Warrant Officer running a 10-person instruction cell at CFLRS in Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu.

The connection is direct. Systematic auditing, risk assessment, process improvement, team leadership: these are the same skills organizations need right now to get AI adoption right. The context changed; the discipline didn't.

---

## My Approach

Every engagement starts with understanding your context. I don't push pre-built solutions.

I think good AI governance needs to be practical (frameworks your team can actually follow), proportionate (controls matched to your risk level, not one-size-fits-all), and built to evolve as your AI maturity and the regulatory environment change. If a governance framework can't grow with you, it's already outdated.
