## From Avionics to Algorithms

I spent 21 years in the Canadian Armed Forces. Started as an avionics technician on CF-188 Hornets, became an electronic warfare tech, then moved into quality management as an AF9000+ Lead Auditor, and finished as a Warrant Officer supervising a 10-person instruction cell at CFLRS in Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu.

It wasn't a random career change. The same skills that kept aircraft systems safe and compliant (systematic auditing, risk assessment, process improvement, and team leadership) are exactly what organizations need right now as they figure out how to adopt AI responsibly.

## My Approach

Every engagement starts with understanding your context, not pushing a pre-built solution. I believe that good AI governance is:

**Practical:** frameworks that your team can actually follow, not shelfware.

**Proportionate:** controls matched to your risk level, not one-size-fits-all.

**Progressive:** designed to evolve as your AI maturity and regulations evolve.
